# Decision Mirror — Governance Charter v1.0

This Charter defines the governance structure,
decision authority, and accountability mechanisms
of the Decision Mirror framework.

Its purpose is to ensure that
no system evolution occurs
without explicit human responsibility.

---

## 1. Purpose

Decision Mirror exists to preserve:

- Human agency
- Informed consent
- Structural veto power
- Traceable accountability
- Reversibility awareness

All governance mechanisms
must serve these principles.

---

## 2. Scope

This Charter applies to:

- All core framework documents
- All derivative implementations
- All official publications
- All governance processes
- All affiliated projects

No component is exempt.

---

## 3. Governance Bodies

### 3.1 Maintainers

Maintainers are responsible for:

- Document integrity
- Version control
- Process enforcement
- Public communication

At least two maintainers
must be active at all times.

---

### 3.2 Reviewers

Reviewers provide independent oversight.

Responsibilities include:

- Evaluating boundary changes
- Assessing governance risks
- Reviewing ethical implications
- Issuing public feedback

Reviewers must not be sole maintainers.

---

### 3.3 Community Advisory Panel (Optional)

If established, this panel:

- Provides external perspectives
- Reviews major amendments
- Represents stakeholder interests

This body has consultative authority only.

---

## 4. Decision Authority

### 4.1 Amendment Process

All structural changes require:

1. Public proposal
2. Written rationale
3. Minimum 14-day review period
4. Maintainer consensus
5. Public record

Silent amendments are prohibited.

---

### 4.2 Emergency Changes

In urgent cases:

- Temporary changes may be applied
- Must be documented within 48 hours
- Must be reviewed retroactively
- May be revoked

Emergency status must be justified.

---

## 5. Transparency Requirements

All governance activities must include:

- Public documentation
- Version history
- Decision rationale
- Named responsible parties

Anonymous authority is prohibited.

---

## 6. Conflict of Interest

All maintainers and reviewers must disclose:

- Financial interests
- Organizational affiliations
- Personal conflicts

Conflicted parties must recuse themselves.

---

## 7. Enforcement Mechanisms

Violations may result in:

- Public warning
- Suspension of privileges
- Removal from governance roles
- Public disclosure

Enforcement actions must be documented.

---

## 8. Community Participation

Community members may:

- Submit proposals
- Request reviews
- Appeal decisions
- Access governance records

Participation must remain open.

---

## 9. Versioning

This Charter follows semantic governance versioning:

- Major: Structural governance change
- Minor: Process refinement
- Patch: Clarification

All versions remain publicly accessible.

---

## 10. Ratification

This Charter is ratified when:

- Approved by active maintainers
- Published publicly
- Linked from README
- Recorded in DECISIONS_LOG

Ratification date must be recorded.

---

All amendments must be listed here.

Format:
